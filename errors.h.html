./errors.h

To report errors, we provide you with a morass of error codes. You can combine this with errno to get a picture of what went wrong. There is no logging available. We surrender immediately at the first error. There is really not much you can do in response to a failure, especially as a library dynamically loaded into a host that used up all its file handles (we only need what, six, is that too much to ask?)

There is no rhyme or reason to these codes. They are numbered according the order in which I thought them up. They are long and verbose to be self documenting.

The last error is obtained using the error method. You can get the last errno associated with the error too. To understand what the errno means, have a look at the source, then have a look at the man pages.
